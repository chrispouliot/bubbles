And yes — this is your anchor code, interacting with the images. Here's the mechanism. Your re-pin runs synchronously inside notify::upper / notify::page-size and sets value = upper - page_size. The message images are width-constrained, so their height is computed height-for-width. At certain widths — exactly the "certain widths" you noticed — widening the window bumps an image's allocated width, which steps its height up. But within the allocation pass that fires the notify, the GtkPicture hasn't re-measured its new taller height yet, so upper is briefly the old, smaller content height. Your handler pins to that stale upper, so value lands ~17px too low and the content sits under-scrolled (down) for that frame. The next layout pass re-measures the image, upper grows, the re-pin fires again and snaps to the true bottom. The ~17px is just the per-step height change of that image as its width ticks over. The down-then-up is stale-pin followed by correct-pin.
Where to look: the notify::upper and notify::page-size → set_value path, plus how your message-image widgets report height-for-width during resize.
